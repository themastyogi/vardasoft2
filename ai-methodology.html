AI Methodology

Where AI actually changes delivery

Not a marketing layer on top of standard ERP work — AI gets used in specific, scoped places where it measurably speeds up or de-risks implementation. Everywhere else, the discipline stays exactly as manual and rigorous as it needs to be.

Ground rules

Three principles before any AI touches your implementation

01

AI accelerates judgment, it doesn't replace it

Every AI-assisted output — a GAP-FIT draft, a data anomaly flag, a generated test case — gets reviewed by a consultant before it becomes a decision. AI produces a faster first pass; a person who has actually implemented BC signs off on it.

02

Scoped where it earns its cost

Not every engagement needs Copilot enablement or anomaly monitoring. We scope AI components based on where the manual effort is genuinely high — not because it's a trend worth billing for.

03

Sure Step and GAP-FIT discipline stays intact

AI doesn't skip phases or shortcut rigor. The same discovery, GAP-FIT, UAT, and cutover structure applies — it just runs faster and catches more, because a first pass gets done by a model instead of by hand.

The program

The AI-augmented delivery phases in detail

Same six-to-seven phase structure as any Sure Step-aligned engagement — here's specifically where and how AI gets layered in.

1
AI-assisted

Accelerated discovery

Traditionally: discovery starts from zero — every pain point has to be surfaced through interviews alone.

Where you have exportable data from your current system, we run AI-assisted analysis on transaction volumes and exception patterns before the first workshop. Interview time then gets spent validating and prioritizing what the data already suggests, instead of fact-finding from scratch.

Output: data-informed current-state document
2
AI-assisted

Intelligent GAP-FIT

Traditionally: every requirement scored manually, one at a time, against a consultant's working knowledge of standard BC functionality.

Requirements get run through an AI-assisted matching pass against BC's standard capability set first, producing an initial Fit/Gap draft quickly. A consultant then reviews and overrides every line — the judgment on what actually matters for your operation still needs someone who's implemented BC before, not a model guessing in isolation.

Output: faster, more consistent first-pass GAP-FIT scorecard
3
AI-assisted

Data migration profiling

Traditionally: data quality issues surface during UAT, or worse, after go-live.

AI-assisted profiling flags duplicates, incomplete records, and formatting inconsistencies in your legacy data before migration runs — with confidence scores on each record so the team knows exactly what needs manual review versus what can be trusted as-is.

Output: pre-migration data quality report
4
Scoped selectively

Copilot-enabled configuration

Traditionally: once BC is configured, using it well depends entirely on end-user training and memory.

Where reporting overhead is high enough to justify it, we configure Microsoft Copilot to answer natural-language questions directly against your BC data — "what's overdue AR by customer" without navigating five reports. This isn't standard on every engagement; we scope it based on actual need, not because it's expected.

Output: reduced ad-hoc reporting load on your team
5
AI-assisted

Augmented UAT

Traditionally: test coverage is limited by how much time a consultant has to think through edge cases manually.

Test scenarios get AI-generated directly from the GAP-FIT requirements document, then reviewed and prioritized by the consultant — surfacing edge cases like unusual quantities, multi-currency transactions, or partial shipments that manual scripting often misses under deadline pressure.

Output: broader UAT coverage without extending the testing timeline
6
AI-assisted

Assisted go-live reconciliation

Traditionally: reconciliation between legacy and new system totals happens manually, line by line, during the highest-pressure window of the whole project.

Automated checks compare legacy and BC totals across key accounts and inventory positions in real time, flagging discrepancies immediately instead of relying on someone manually cross-checking spreadsheets during cutover.

Output: faster, more reliable go-live sign-off
7
Scoped selectively

Continuous monitoring

Traditionally: post-launch support is entirely reactive — someone notices a problem, then files a ticket.

Where scoped, we set up anomaly monitoring on key data points — unusual stock adjustments, unexpected GL postings — so issues get flagged before they compound into a larger cleanup. Combined with Copilot enablement, this also tends to cut down basic "how do I run this report" ticket volume over time.

Output: fewer post-launch surprises, lower support load
