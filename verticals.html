Industries we know from the inside

Not generalist ERP — deep, repeat experience in the operations that are hardest to configure well.

Food & beverage

Perishability, batch accountability, and recall-readiness make F&B one of the least forgiving verticals to implement wrong. Standard inventory logic doesn't hold up against shelf life, catch-weight, and formula-driven production.

Lot & batch traceability Catch-weight inventory Recipe & formula management Shelf-life tracking Recall readiness

Automotive DMS

Dealer networks run on systems that were never designed to talk to each other — DMS, OEM portals, and finance. The work is less about configuring Business Central and more about making it the connective layer.

Dealer management workflows Parts & service integration OEM-facing reporting Warranty claim tracking

Manufacturing

Production data that reaches finance days late is a planning problem in disguise. The goal is tying shop-floor activity directly into financial reporting, so decisions are made on what's happening now, not what happened last week.

Production order management Capacity planning Shop-floor data capture Real-time cost tracking
